About

The story behind Mina Son Collective

Hi everyone, my name is Mina Son, and welcome to Mina Son Collective. I’m currently a junior at Arizona State University studying AI in Business. Beyond academics, I’m a Korean American woman whose passions live in creative spaces like music, design, and content creation.

I grew up in Colorado and moved to Arizona during my senior year of high school. Initially, I hadn’t planned on attending ASU. I was accepted into three of my dream schools: UC Irvine, UC Berkeley, and UC Davis. However, the idea of relocating again so soon after settling in Arizona felt overwhelming. At the same time, I was dealing with unexpected health complications, which ultimately guided me toward staying here and starting my journey at ASU.

There's a common pressure for students to have their entire path figured out before even stepping into college. Even once you’re here, it can feel like the professional world only sees a narrow version of who you are.

My journey really began in my early years when I discovered music and fell in love with it. While I’ve always dreamed of pursuing music as a career, I also recognized that it doesn’t always guarantee stability. By my junior year of high school, my priorities shifted from passion to financial security, and for a time, I believed money itself had become my passion. It sounds ironic, but I’ve come to realize that true passion attracts success, while chasing money alone can push it away.

Now, as a college student studying business with a focus on technology, I’ve noticed how much weight is placed on resumes. They highlight credentials, skills, education, and experience, yet they often fail to capture the full complexity of a person. In a world where GPAs, extracurriculars, and achievements attempt to define us, we are still so much more than a list of qualifications.

That realization is exactly why I created Mina Son Collective, a space designed to present me as a person, not a product. It’s something I can include alongside my LinkedIn or resume, staying within professional boundaries while offering a deeper look into who I am beyond the page.

Arizona State University student, Sister of aKDPhi, Founder and President of the Billionaire Blueprint Club, and Founder of Mina Son Collective.
